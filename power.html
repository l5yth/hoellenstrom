Math Reference — Formulas Used Throughout

Every number in this document derives from one or more of the following four formulas. Annotations in each section show the substituted values.

① Apparent Power (kVA) from 3-phase current
S = √3 × UL × I [VA]
UL = line-to-line voltage = 400 V (Germany, 3-phase) · I = line current in Amperes
√3 ≈ 1.732 — arises because 3-phase line voltage is √3 times the phase voltage
② Active Power (kW) from apparent power
P = S × cos φ [W]
cos φ = power factor. Resistive loads (heaters) = 1.0. Inductive (motors) ≈ 0.7–0.85.
Event electronics (laptops, chargers, LED drivers, PA amplifiers): cos φ ≈ 0.85–0.95.
This plan uses cos φ = 0.90 as the working assumption for mixed event electronics.
Important: S (kVA) is what the fuse/cable sees. P (kW) is what the equipment actually uses.
③ Voltage drop over a 3-phase cable run
ΔU (V) = √3 × I × L × (ρ / A)
I = current [A] · L = one-way cable length [m] · ρ = resistivity of copper = 0.0175 Ω·mm²/m
A = cable cross-section [mm²] · ΔU% = (ΔU / 400V) × 100
VDE 0100-718 (temporary event installations): recommended ΔU ≤ 5% for the full circuit.
Note: modern switching power supplies (laptops, chargers, LED drivers) tolerate ΔU up to ~15%
because they operate on 100–240V AC. Analogue audio and older equipment is more sensitive.
④ Diversity factor — realistic load from rated capacity
Pactual = Pinstalled × d
d = diversity factor: the fraction of installed capacity drawing power simultaneously.
Camping loads (phone chargers, laptop chargers): d ≈ 0.15–0.20
Event tents (talks, PA, lighting running together): d ≈ 0.70–0.85
Glamping tents (small appliances, evening peak): d ≈ 0.25–0.40
Note on the "22kW for 32A" figure used in earlier revisions (rev 0.1–0.3) That figure was S (apparent power) = √3 × 400V × 32A = 22.2 kVA, not P (active watts). Corrected: at cos φ = 0.90, P = 22.2 kVA × 0.90 = 20.0 kW usable per 32A 3-phase circuit. At cos φ = 0.80 (conservative): P = 22.2 × 0.80 = 17.8 kW. All area calculations in this revision use cos φ = 0.90.

Voltage Drop Calculations — Step by Step

Formula: ΔU (V) = √3 × I × L × (ρ/A) = 1.732 × I × L × (0.0175/A) → ΔU% = ΔU/400V × 100
Run 1: Rosa Haus → Glamping (250m, 32A, 16mm²) ΔU = 1.732 × 32 × 250 × (0.0175 / 16) = 1.732 × 32 × 250 × 0.001094 = 1.732 × 32 × 0.2734 = 1.732 × 8.75 = 15.15 V ΔU% = 15.15 / 400 × 100 = 3.79% ✓ within 5% At typical I = 14A: ΔU = 1.732 × 14 × 250 × 0.001094 = 6.6V = 1.65% ✓
Run 2: Rosa Haus → Self-Camping (350m, 32A, 16mm²) ΔU = 1.732 × 32 × 350 × (0.0175 / 16) = 1.732 × 32 × 350 × 0.001094 = 1.732 × 32 × 0.3828 = 1.732 × 12.25 = 21.2 V ΔU% = 21.2 / 400 × 100 = 5.3% — marginally above 5% VDE guideline at full rated current At typical I = 14A: ΔU = 1.732 × 14 × 350 × 0.001094 = 9.3V = 2.3% ✓ The 5.3% overshoot occurs only at full 32A. Camping loads rarely reach full rated current (diversity d ≈ 0.15). For strict compliance at rated current: use 25mm² → ΔU = 1.732 × 32 × 350 × (0.0175/25) = 13.6V = 3.4% ✓ Recommendation: 16mm² is acceptable given load type. Discuss 25mm² with electrician if strict compliance is required.
Run 3: Rosa Haus → Small Camp Tents (100m, 32A) At 4mm²: ΔU = 1.732 × 32 × 100 × (0.0175/4) = 1.732 × 32 × 0.4375 = 24.2V = 6.1% — above 5% At 6mm²: ΔU = 1.732 × 32 × 100 × (0.0175/6) = 1.732 × 32 × 0.2917 = 16.1V = 4.0% ✓ Use 6mm² for strict compliance. 4mm² is acceptable at typical load (I ≈ 14A → ΔU = 10.6V = 2.6%).
Run 4: Gasthaus → Hacker Tent (100m, 32A, 6mm²) ΔU = 1.732 × 32 × 100 × (0.0175/6) = 1.732 × 32 × 0.2917 = 16.1V = 4.0% ✓ At typical I = 22A: ΔU = 1.732 × 22 × 100 × 0.002917 = 11.1V = 2.8% ✓
Run 5: Gasthaus → Forest Locations (200m, 32A, 16mm²) ΔU = 1.732 × 32 × 200 × (0.0175/16) = 1.732 × 32 × 0.21875 = 12.1V = 3.0% ✓ At typical I = 18A: ΔU = 1.732 × 18 × 200 × 0.001094 = 6.8V = 1.7% ✓
